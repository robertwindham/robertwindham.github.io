About me

I grew up in the beautiful rolling hills of Wartrace, TN, and whether I was fishing with my dad in nearby streams or exploring the forests behind our house, I always felt most at peace in nature. After taking my high school AP Environmental Science course, I became invested in conservation ecology, continuining to study biology throughout my undergrad at the University of Tennessee, Chattanooga. Currently, I am a graduate student at Austin Peay State University studying ecology, biology, and land conservation. In my free time, I enjoy hiking, birding, spending time with family, and going to the movies. Work & Volunteer Experience

After graduating college in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, I worked as a field technician for Land Managment Logistics conducting Hellbender Salamander surveys in north eastern TN. For two summers, I also interned at the Tennessee River Gorge Trust, a non profit land trust based in Chattanooga focused on the conservation, preservation, and management of more than 17,000 acres across the greater Chattanooga area. Since attending Austin Peay State University, I have volunteered at both the Warner Parks Bird Research Program in Nashville, TN and the South Eastern Grasslands Initiative in Clarksville, TN.
