About me

I grew up in the beautiful rolling hills of Wartrace, TN. Whether fishing with my dad in nearby streams or exploring the forests behind our house as a child, I always felt most at peace in nature. After taking my high school AP Environmental Science course, I became invested in conservation ecology, continuining to study biology throughout my undergrad at the University of Tennessee, Chattanooga. Currently, I am a graduate student at Austin Peay State University studying ecology, biology, and land conservation. In my free time, I enjoy hiking, birding, spending time with family, and going to the movies. Work & Volunteer Experience

After graduating college in the midst of the COVID-19 pandemic, I worked as a field technician for Land Managment Logistics conducting Hellbender Salamander surveys in north eastern TN. For the summers of 2022 and 2023, I also interned at the Tennessee River Gorge Trust, a non profit land trust based in Chattanooga focused on the conservation, preservation, and management of more than 17,000 acres across the greater Chattanooga area.

While attending graduate school at Austin Peay State University, I have worked as a GTA, teaching Anatomy and Physiology 1 labs. During 2023 and 2024, I have also volunteered at both the Warner Parks Bird Research Program in Nashville, TN and the South Eastern Grasslands Initiative in Clarksville, TN.
